# Idea: AI Tutor for Mexican *Secundaria* Entrance Exam Prep

## Problem statement

Today, 6th-grade students in Mexico preparing for *secundaria* entrance exams struggle to assess and close gaps in their mastery of exam topics because human tutors are out of reach for most families and parents can't fill the gap, which leads to underperformance on a high-stakes placement exam. They need personalized practice and mastery feedback that works without a knowledgeable adult nearby.

## Target user

6th-grade students in Mexico (ages 11–12) preparing for a private or selective public *secundaria* entrance exam, whose parents can't afford ongoing private tutoring and don't have the time or subject knowledge to coach them through it themselves.

## Why now

Post-pandemic learning loss has widened the prep gap at the same time that general-purpose AI has become capable enough to deliver personalized, subject-accurate tutoring at near-zero marginal cost.

## Non-goals

1. **Not a general-purpose study app.** Scope is specifically *secundaria* entrance exam prep — not homework help, not high school prep, not English learning.
2. **Not optimizing for time-on-app.** We will not chase engagement-hours as a success metric; a student who masters the material in less time is a better outcome, not a worse one.
3. **Not replacing human tutors for families who can afford them.** The wedge is the underserved segment; we are not building a premium tutor-replacement product for middle/upper-class families.

## Success hypothesis

**If** we give target students a personalized practice and mastery-feedback loop, **then** within 8–12 weeks of consistent use we will see measurable week-over-week improvement on exam-aligned diagnostics, and a meaningful lift in actual exam placement rates versus a comparable non-user baseline — without students needing 1–2 hours/day of app time to get there.
